About Me

I am a Principle Software Engineer at Liberty Mutual working in the Corporate Technology AI/ML Platform Engineering Team. I develop applications that enable data scientists and engineers to execute analytics, machine learning, and big data initiatives across all Liberty business units.

Previously, I was a Senior Solutions Engineer at CodeSignal. My focus was on the implementation and adoption of assessment frameworks within the context of the technical hiring processes. Before that I was an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Idaho State University, focusing on Empirical Software Engineering. I completed my Ph.D. in December 2021 at Montana State University with Dr. Clemente Izurieta as my adviser. Before joining ISU, I graduated with a B.S. in Computer Science (2011), a B.A. in Philosophy (2011), an M.S. in Computer Science (2014), and a Graduate Certificate in Applied Statistics (2014) from Montana State University.

My current research interests include Empirical Software Engineering, Software Quality Analysis, Software Testing, Multi-Lingual Parsing, and Software Process Simulation. I am branching out into Design Science and its applications to Extended Reality. For more information about some of my research and other projects, visit my research and projects page.
